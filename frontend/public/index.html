How ROI is Computed

ROI (Return on Investment) is calculated as the savings during the failure-to-replacement period divided by the AT repair or replacement cost, expressed as a percentage.

For example, if savings during the failure-to-replacement period are $10,000 and the AT repair cost is $5,000:

ROI = (10,000 / 5,000) * 100 = 200%
